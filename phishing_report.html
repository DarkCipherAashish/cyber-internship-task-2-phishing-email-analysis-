Phishing Email Analysis Report

Phishing Indicators Found

  • Sender Spoofing: The email is from "security@hdfcbank-support.in", a spoofed address with a non-official domain (legitimate is "hdfcbank.com") and added "-support" to deceive users.
  • Header Discrepancies: Analysis with an online header analyzer (e.g., MX Toolbox) shows the email originated from an IP (e.g., 198.51.100.10) not associated with HDFC Bank’s servers, indicating manipulation (see screenshot_headers.png).
  • Suspicious Link: The URL "https://hdfconline-login.com/verify-now" mismatches the official "hdfcbank.com" and points to a potential phishing site when hovered over.
  • Urgent/Threatening Language: Phrases like "Urgent," "suspicious login attempts," and "permanently disabled" create pressure to act hastily.
  • Suspicious Attachment: The "account_recovery.exe" file suggests a malware payload, a common tactic in real phishing attacks.
  • Spelling/Grammar Errors: The informal "Regards" and abrupt tone shift indicate unprofessional scripting typical of phishing emails.

Summary

This email displays multiple phishing characteristics, including a spoofed sender, mismatched URL, urgent language, and a malicious attachment. These align with social engineering techniques to exploit trust and fear, key threats in email security.

Interview Question Responses

  • 1. What is phishing? A cyberattack where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords or bank details.
  • 2. How to identify a phishing email? Check the sender domain, hover over links, look for urgency or errors, and analyze headers.
  • 3. What is email spoofing? Faking the "From" address to appear as a legitimate source.
  • 4. Why are phishing emails dangerous? They can lead to financial loss, identity theft, or malware infection.
  • 5. How can you verify the sender’s authenticity? Contact the organization via the official website (e.g., hdfcbank.com) or phone number.
  • 6. What tools can analyze email headers? Free tools like MX Toolbox or Google’s Message Header Analyzer.
  • 7. What actions should be taken on suspected phishing emails? Do not click/open; report to IT or hdfc@phishing.report; delete.
  • 8. How do attackers use social engineering in phishing? They use urgency or trust to manipulate users into revealing information or clicking links.
